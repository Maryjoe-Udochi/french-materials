SECTION 1

The Level-Up Staircase — Connectors

Connectors are the glue words that hold your writing together. Without them, your sentences are like beads without a string — ideas everywhere! Using C1/C2 connectors instead of B1 ones is one of the fastest ways to boost your score.

SECTION 4

Tâche 1 — Your Go-To Words (Short Message)

📋
What is it? A short informal or semi-formal message — email, note, text. You write to a friend, neighbour, colleague, or stranger about an everyday situation.
Register: Informal (friend/family) → Salut ! / À bientôt ! | Semi-formal (stranger) → Bonjour / Cordialement
Must include: Address ALL parts of the prompt · Clear subject line · Proper greeting & sign-off · All requested info (date, place, price, etc.)

SECTION 5

Tâche 2 — Your Go-To Words (Formal Letter)

📋
What is it? A formal or semi-formal letter to someone in authority — employer, school, landlord, newspaper, airline, administration.
Register: FORMAL — like wearing a suit. Use vous, no contractions, no slang.
Opening: Madame, Monsieur,   Closing: ...mes cordiales salutations.
Must include: City + date · Clear Objet: line · Formal opening · Context paragraph · Details paragraph · Request paragraph · Formal sign-off

SECTION 6

Tâche 3 — Your Go-To Words (Essay & Debate)

📋
What is it? You read 2 short documents and write: (1) a synthesis of both, (2) your opinion with arguments. You must give it a title.
Register: VERY FORMAL — like an academic essay. Structured, serious, no slang.
Structure: Introduction → Synthesis → Arguments → Conclusion
Must include: A meaningful TITLE · Introduction (the debate question) · Synthesis of both documents · Your opinion with 2+ arguments · Conclusion
